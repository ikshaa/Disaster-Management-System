The Problem

During major disasters — earthquakes, floods, fires — emergency services receive thousands of reports simultaneously. Human dispatchers cannot quickly determine which calls are life-threatening. Critical cases get delayed. Lives are lost.

How It Works
1
Citizen submits a report — plain text description, GPS pin, and an optional photo of the disaster site
2
DistilBERT NLP classifies the text into one of 7 emergency severity levels with a confidence score
3
ResNet50 vision analyzes the photo — detects fire, flood, collapsed building, traffic incident, or normal
4
Priority score is computed (NLP + Vision + GPS cluster density within 500m radius)
5
Responder dashboard updates live via WebSocket — ranked map and list, one click to dispatch
Global Disaster Context

Each year, over 400 natural disasters affect 160+ million people worldwide. The 2010 Haiti earthquake killed 230,000 — many due to delayed rescue coordination. The 2023 Turkey–Syria earthquake displaced 5.9 million people within 48 hours, overwhelming every local emergency system.

Traditional 911 systems rely on human operators to classify and route calls one by one. During a mass-casualty event, this single-threaded triage becomes the bottleneck that costs lives.

Rescue-AI targets the critical first 72 hours — when AI-assisted triage can process hundreds of reports per minute, ensuring that the most life-threatening situations reach responders first, not last.

Project Significance

Rescue-AI is the first open-source disaster triage system combining real-time NLP + computer vision + GPS clustering in a single deployable stack — with a built-in offline mesh fallback for disaster zones where infrastructure has been destroyed.

Unlike commercial CAD (Computer-Aided Dispatch) systems costing $100k+, Rescue-AI runs on commodity hardware, is fully open-source, and can be deployed in under 10 minutes using Docker.

Key Contributions
🔀
Hybrid NLP Classifier
DistilBERT fine-tuned on 22k CrisisNLP tweets + a rule-based keyword layer. A smart override prevents the model from downgrading clearly critical reports — combining statistical power with interpretable rules.
📍
Location Risk Amplification
Haversine distance formula dynamically clusters GPS reports within a 500m radius. Three or more reports from the same block raise the location risk score from 0 to 8, amplifying urgency automatically without human intervention.
🌐
Online & Offline in One System
The same AI pipeline, database schema, and dashboard serve both direct internet submissions and offline mesh relay reports. The relay node is independently deployable with no AI imports or GPU — runs on a $35 Raspberry Pi.
🧾
Explainable AI Reasoning
Every report stores a full JSON reasoning trace: NLP class + confidence, image damage class, location cluster count, and the complete score breakdown. Responders can audit every AI decision — building trust in automated triage.

Why a Mesh Relay Matters
💥
In major disasters, cell towers are often the first infrastructure to fail. The 2010 Haiti earthquake knocked out 60% of mobile networks within minutes. The 2023 Turkey earthquake left entire provinces without cellular for days. A disaster response system that depends solely on internet connectivity fails exactly when it is needed most.
📡
How the relay works: A relay node (any laptop or Raspberry Pi) creates a closed wireless hotspot with no internet connection — like a router with the ISP cable unplugged. Citizens' phones connect to this device-to-device network and submit reports through a browser. All data stays on the relay's local storage. When the relay eventually reaches the AI hub and gains internet, it syncs all queued reports in one operation.
🧠
Same AI, same results: Every synced report runs through the identical NLP classifier, vision assessor, and priority engine as a directly submitted report. Responders see the same AI reasoning, the same score breakdown, and the same ranked dashboard — with no indication of how the report arrived.
🔒
Fully independent design: The relay node has zero imports from the AI backend and requires no GPU or ML libraries. It can run on a $35 Raspberry Pi, making it practical to deploy relay nodes across a disaster zone before an event even begins.
